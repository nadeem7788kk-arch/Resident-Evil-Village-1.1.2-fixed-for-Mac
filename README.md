Mac port by Capcom
The macOS edition is published by CAPCOM Co., Ltd. on the Mac App Store for Apple Silicon Macs (M1 and later). It is a native port, not a Feral Interactive release. According to Capcom’s Mac product pages, supported systems include macOS 12.0 (Monterey) and later; Intel-based Macs are not listed as compatible.

Gameplay and content
You explore the village, manage inventory and crafting, fight Lycans and bosses, and progress Ethan’s story after Resident Evil 7 biohazard. The base game supports extensive graphics options on Mac, including MetalFX upscaling modes described in community and technical coverage of the port. Optional in-app purchases cover the Winters’ Expansion DLC (Third Person Mode, The Mercenaries — Additional Orders, Shadows of Rose), Trauma Pack items, and an Extra Content Shop voucher, as listed on the App Store product page.

Licensing, connectivity, and saves
The App Store listing states that a network connection is required when starting the application (Capcom adjusted startup rules in later updates; verify on your installed build). Game Center achievements are supported. From version 1.1.2 onward, the Mac build can upload and download save data via iCloud Drive; Capcom documents that saves cannot be transferred between macOS and iOS installs. The game does not support Universal Purchase across Apple platforms.

Storage and controllers
Install size is roughly 34–35 GB; Capcom recommends having about twice that free space during installation and downloading on a stable connection. Controller play is recommended; keyboard and mouse are supported. A free portion of the base game may be playable before an in-app purchase unlocks the full campaign, per store terms.

Version 1.1.2 in context
Build 1.1.2 (October 2023 on the Mac App Store version history) focused on cloud save support and display settings. Later official updates (for example 1.1.4 in February 2024) addressed iCloud upload failures on certain OS versions and a Shopping Addict achievement bug. If you need those fixes, use a current App Store update rather than staying on 1.1.2.

Compared with PC and console
The Mac port targets high visual quality on Apple Silicon but, according to widely reported technical comparisons, omits hardware ray tracing present on some PC and current-console versions. Cross-play and cross-save with Steam or console editions are not part of this Mac App Store product.
